On January 9, 2007 Steve Jobs "will" introduce the first iPhone. The iPhone is a combination of three products —a revolutionary mobile phone, a widescreen iPod® with touch controls, and a breakthrough Internet communications device with desktop-class email, web browsing, searching and maps—into one small and lightweight handheld device.

On January 9, 2007, Steve Jobs took out his iPhone and dialed a Starbucks in San Francisco. It was the first public iPhone call. Nobody will ever foget that moment.
"Good morning," answered the polite voice of employee Ying Hang "Hannah" Zhang. "How may I help you?"
"Yes, I'd like to order 4,000 lattes to go, please," Jobs said, grinning. "No, just kidding. Wrong number. Goodbye!"
